# Top 5 State-Mandated Retirement Program Markets

**Strategic Market Brief — February 2026**

---

## Executive Summary

Twenty U.S. states have enacted state-mandated or state-sponsored retirement savings programs for private-sector employers. The five markets below represent the highest-impact opportunity for products and services targeting compliance, payroll integration, plan administration, and retirement plan alternatives (e.g., 401(k) providers). They are ranked by a composite of state economy size, breadth of employer mandate, enforcement posture, compliance urgency, and total addressable employer population.

---

## 1. California — CalSavers

**Market Impact: Highest**

### Program Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Program Name | CalSavers |
| Governing Law | SB 1234 (2012, 2016); SB 1126 (2022 expansion) |
| Plan Type | Roth IRA (Traditional IRA option available for high earners) |
| Default Contribution | 5% of gross pay; auto-escalates 1%/year to 8% cap |
| Employer Contributions | Prohibited |
| Employee Opt-Out | Yes, within 30 days of enrollment; can re-enroll anytime |
| Program Administrator | CalSavers / Ascensus (recordkeeper) |
| Participant Fees | ~0.325%–0.49% expense ratio + $17/year admin fee |

### Employer Threshold & Eligibility

- 1+ employees (all California employers with at least one W-2 employee)
- Must have been in business and reported employees to EDD in the prior calendar year
- Does not currently offer a qualified retirement plan (401(k), 403(b), SEP, SIMPLE IRA, etc.)
- **Exempt:** sole proprietors/owner-only businesses, government entities, religious organizations, tribal organizations

### Compliance Deadlines (All Passed)

| Employer Size | Registration Deadline |
|---|---|
| 100+ employees | September 30, 2020 |
| 50+ employees | June 30, 2021 |
| 5+ employees | June 30, 2022 |
| 1–4 employees | December 31, 2025 |

> All employers are now in active enforcement. Late registrations are still being accepted, but penalties may already apply.

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

- **$250** per eligible employee if non-compliance exceeds 90 days after notice
- **Additional $500** per eligible employee if non-compliance exceeds 180 days after notice
- **Total potential:** $750 per employee in the first year; $500/employee for each subsequent year of continued non-compliance

### Employer Reporting & Compliance Obligations

1. Register via the CalSavers Employer Portal using EIN, CA payroll tax number, and CalSavers access code
2. Upload employee roster — all eligible employees (age 18+, California W-2 wages)
3. New hire enrollment — must add new employees within 30 days of hire
4. Facilitate payroll deductions — first contribution due on the first pay date occurring 30 days after roster upload
5. Ongoing roster maintenance — update employee information, manage opt-outs, add employees turning 18
6. No tax reporting burden — IRA trustee (not employer) files Form 5498 with the IRS
7. No fiduciary liability — employers are not fiduciaries and bear no investment liability

### Why #1

California is the largest state economy with the broadest mandate (1+ employees). An estimated 400,000+ businesses with 1–5 employees entered the compliance window in 2025. The penalty structure is aggressive and enforcement is active. The sheer volume of affected small businesses makes this the single largest addressable market.

## 2. New York — Secure Choice Savings Program

**Market Impact: Very High — Active Greenfield**

### Program Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Program Name | New York State Secure Choice Savings Program |
| Governing Law | NY General Business Law, Article 43 (enacted 2021, launched Oct 2025) |
| Plan Type | Roth IRA |
| Default Contribution | 3% of gross wages |
| Employer Contributions | Prohibited |
| Employee Opt-Out | Yes, at any time |
| Program Administrator | Secure Choice Savings Program Board / third-party administrator |
| Employer Fees | None |

### Employer Threshold & Eligibility

- 10+ employees in New York State during the prior calendar year
- In business for at least 2 years
- Has not offered a qualified retirement plan in the preceding 2 years
- Part-time and seasonal employees count toward the 10-employee threshold
- Employers with existing qualified plans must still certify their exemption via the portal

### Compliance Deadlines (Rolling — 2026)

| Employer Size | Registration Deadline |
|---|---|
| 30+ employees | March 18, 2026 |
| 15–29 employees | May 15, 2026 |
| 10–14 employees | July 15, 2026 |

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

- As of February 2026, no formal penalty schedule has been published
- The law authorizes civil penalties for failure to register or certify exemption
- Industry observers expect penalties comparable to peer states ($250+ per employee), with escalation over time
- The state has signaled an education-first approach initially, with enforcement expected to follow

### Employer Reporting & Compliance Obligations

1. Register or certify exemption via the Secure Choice employer portal using EIN and unique access code (mailed/emailed by the state)
2. Submit employee data — name, SSN/ITIN, date of birth, contact information for all eligible employees (18+, employed in NY)
3. Facilitate payroll deductions — post-tax Roth IRA deductions per employee election or 3% default
4. Distribute program information to employees (materials provided by the state)
5. Enroll new hires in a timely manner
6. No investment advice — employers must not advise employees on the program; refer to program website or financial advisor
7. No tax reporting burden — IRA trustee files Form 5498
8. No fiduciary liability

### Why #2

New York is the second-largest state economy and this is a brand-new mandate with deadlines actively rolling out in 2026. Millions of private-sector workers are affected. Businesses are in the decision-making window right now, choosing between the state program and private alternatives. This creates an enormous greenfield opportunity for 401(k) providers, payroll companies, and compliance services.

## 3. Illinois — Secure Choice

**Market Impact: High — Mature, Proven Market**

### Program Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Program Name | Illinois Secure Choice |
| Governing Law | Illinois Secure Choice Savings Program Act (820 ILCS 80/) |
| Plan Type | Roth IRA (Traditional IRA available for income-ineligible participants) |
| Default Contribution | 5% of gross pay |
| Employer Contributions | Prohibited |
| Employee Opt-Out | Yes, within 30 days of enrollment; can re-enroll anytime |
| Program Administrator | Illinois State Treasurer / Ascensus (recordkeeper) |
| Participant Fees | ~0.75% annual fee on assets |

### Employer Threshold & Eligibility

- 5+ employees in every quarter of the previous calendar year
- In business for at least 2 years
- Does not offer or contribute to a qualified retirement plan
- Applies to for-profit and nonprofit employers
- Also open voluntarily to non-mandated employers and self-employed individuals

### Compliance Deadlines (All Passed)

| Employer Size | Registration Deadline |
|---|---|
| 25+ employees | November 1, 2018 |
| 16–24 employees | July 1, 2019 |
| 5–15 employees | November 1, 2023 |

> All deadlines have passed. Enforcement is active through the Illinois Department of Revenue (IDOR).

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

- **Tier I:** $250 per eligible employee — first calendar year of non-compliance (assessed 90+ days after notice)
- **Tier II:** $500 per eligible employee — each subsequent calendar year of non-compliance
- Employers receive a Notice of Proposed Assessment (IDOR-2P-NT) and have 120 days to come into compliance or request a hearing
- Coming into compliance within the 120-day window avoids the proposed penalty

### Employer Reporting & Compliance Obligations

1. Register at employer.ilsecurechoice.com using employer access code
2. Upload eligible employee roster — all employees age 18+
3. Auto-enroll employees — employees who do not opt out within 30 days are enrolled at 5% default
4. Facilitate payroll deductions — deduct and remit contributions each pay period
5. Enroll new hires — within 30 days
6. Maintain employee records — update roster for terminations, new hires, address changes
7. No fiduciary liability — employer is not a plan sponsor or fiduciary
8. Exempt employers must file exemption at employer.ilsecurechoice.com/exempt

### Why #3

Illinois is the pioneer of state auto-IRA programs (launched 2018) and has the most mature enforcement infrastructure. As of September 2025, over 7,600 employers were actively submitting payroll deductions. The program manages ~$292 million in assets. The proven compliance cycle and aggressive IDOR enforcement create sustained, recurring demand for compliance solutions and private-plan alternatives.

## 4. New Jersey — RetireReady NJ

**Market Impact: High — Enforcement Ramping**

### Program Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Program Name | RetireReady NJ |
| Governing Law | NJ Secure Choice Savings Program Act (signed 2019, launched 2024) |
| Plan Type | Roth IRA (Traditional IRA option available) |
| Default Contribution | 3% of gross pay |
| Employer Contributions | Prohibited |
| Employee Opt-Out | Yes, at any time |
| Program Administrator | NJ Secure Choice Savings Board / third-party administrator |
| Participant Fees | ~0.75% annual fee on assets |

### Employer Threshold & Eligibility

- 25+ employees (current law; pending legislation — Assembly Bill A5358 — would reduce to 1+)
- Employees counted: age 18+, reside in or employed in NJ, wages subject to NJ income tax
- In business for at least 2 years
- Does not currently offer a qualified retirement plan
- Both for-profit and nonprofit employers are covered
- Exempt employers must still certify their exemption with RetireReady NJ

### Compliance Deadlines

| Employer Size | Registration Deadline | Penalty Enforcement Begins |
|---|---|---|
| 40+ employees | September 15, 2024 | June 15, 2025 |
| 25–39 employees | November 15, 2024 | August 15, 2025 |

> The portal continues to accept late registrations.

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Year of Non-Compliance | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Year 1 | Written warning |
| Year 2 | $100 per employee |
| Years 3–4 | $250 per employee |
| Year 5+ | $500 per employee |

### Employer Reporting & Compliance Obligations

1. Register at the RetireReady NJ portal or certify exemption using state-provided access code
2. Enroll eligible employees — automatically enroll within 30 days of enrollment deadline (existing employees) or within 3 months of hire (new employees)
3. Facilitate payroll deductions — collect and remit employee contributions per pay period
4. Distribute enrollment materials — provide state-prepared employee notification forms
5. Track opt-outs — maintain records of employees who elect not to participate
6. No employer contributions required
7. No fiduciary liability

### Why #4

New Jersey has a large, dense small-business economy and the 25+ employee threshold captures a significant mid-market segment with higher per-account revenue potential. Penalties are beginning to take effect in 2025, driving urgency. The pending A5358 legislation to expand the mandate to all employers with 1+ employees would dramatically increase the market. The proximity to New York creates cross-selling opportunities for multi-state compliance solutions.

## 5. Minnesota — Secure Choice Retirement Program

**Market Impact: High — Largest Greenfield Opportunity**

### Program Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Program Name | Minnesota Secure Choice Retirement Program (MNSCRP) |
| Governing Law | Minn. Stat. §§ 187.01–187.11 (enacted May 2023) |
| Plan Type | Roth IRA (default); Traditional IRA option available |
| Default Contribution | 5% of gross pay; auto-escalates 1%/year to 8% cap |
| Employer Contributions | Prohibited |
| Employee Opt-Out | Yes, at any time |
| Program Administrator | MN Secure Choice Board of Directors / private recordkeeper |
| Employer Fees | None |

### Employer Threshold & Eligibility

- 5+ employees (W-2, age 18+)
- In business for at least 1 year (12 months)
- Does not offer or contribute to a qualified retirement plan, and has not done so in the preceding 12 months
- Applies to for-profit and nonprofit employers
- Applies even if employees live outside Minnesota

### Compliance Deadlines (Rolling — 2026–2028)

| Employer Size | Registration Deadline |
|---|---|
| Soft launch (all sizes voluntary) | January 16 – March 30, 2026 |
| 100+ employees | June 30, 2026 |
| 50–99 employees | December 31, 2026 |
| 10–24 employees | December 31, 2027 |
| 5–9 employees | June 30, 2028 |

> Employers receive communications with unique access codes beginning the week of January 19, 2026.

### Penalties for Non-Compliance *(Recommended by Board; Final Schedule Pending)*

| Year of Non-Compliance | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Year 1 | Written warning (required by statute before penalties) |
| Year 2 | $200 per employee (max $6,000) |
| Year 3 | $300 per employee (no max) |
| Ongoing years | $500 per employee (no max) |

> **Additional:** If an employer fails to both enroll employees and distribute required information, penalties are doubled. Employers who fail to remit withheld payroll deductions within 10 days after demand may face misdemeanor charges.

### Employer Reporting & Compliance Obligations

1. Register or file exemption via the self-service online portal (launching January 2026) using EIN and access code
2. Distribute program information to employees — at least 30 days before the first paycheck from which contributions could be deducted (materials provided by the Board)
3. Auto-enroll eligible employees at 5% default Roth IRA contribution
4. Facilitate payroll deductions — deduct and remit contributions; the program will interface with existing payroll software
5. Maintain employee records — keep eligibility data current
6. No fiduciary liability — employers are not fiduciaries and not liable for investment outcomes
7. Estimated admin burden — approximately 7 hours/week for contribution management, record updates, and reporting (per state estimates)

### Why #5

Minnesota is the most significant net-new market entering the compliance cycle in 2026. Over 700,000 Minnesota employees currently lack access to a workplace retirement plan. The soft launch began January 16, 2026, meaning businesses are actively evaluating options for the first time. The multi-year phased rollout (through mid-2028) creates a sustained sales window. First-mover advantage for payroll integrators, 401(k) providers, and compliance platforms is substantial. The 5-employee threshold ensures broad coverage across the small-business landscape.

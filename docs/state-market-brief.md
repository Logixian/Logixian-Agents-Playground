## 1. California — CalSavers

**Market Impact: Highest**
**Governing Law:** SB 1234 (2012, 2016); SB 1126 (2022 expansion)

### Program
- **Plan Type:** Roth IRA (Traditional IRA available for high earners)
- **Default Contribution:** 5% of gross pay; auto-escalates 1%/year to 8% cap
- **Employer Contributions:** Prohibited
- **Employee Opt-Out:** Yes, within 30 days of enrollment; can re-enroll anytime
- **Administrator:** CalSavers / Ascensus (recordkeeper)
- **Participant Fees:** ~0.325%–0.49% expense ratio + $17/year admin fee

### Eligibility Rules
- **Threshold:** 1+ employees (all CA employers with at least one W-2 employee)
- Must have been in business and reported employees to EDD in prior calendar year
- Does **not** currently offer a qualified retirement plan (401k, 403b, SEP, SIMPLE IRA, etc.)
- **Exempt:** sole proprietors/owner-only businesses, government entities, religious organizations, tribal organizations

### Compliance Deadlines (All Passed)
| Employer Size | Registration Deadline |
|--------------|----------------------|
| 100+ employees | September 30, 2020 |
| 50+ employees | June 30, 2021 |
| 5+ employees | June 30, 2022 |
| 1–4 employees | December 31, 2025 |

All employers are now in active enforcement. Late registrations still accepted; penalties may apply.

### Penalties
- **$250/eligible employee** if non-compliance exceeds 90 days after notice
- **Additional $500/eligible employee** if non-compliance exceeds 180 days after notice
- **Total max: $750/employee** in year 1; $500/employee each subsequent year

### Employer Obligations
1. Register via CalSavers Employer Portal (EIN, CA payroll tax number, CalSavers access code)
2. Upload employee roster — all eligible employees (age 18+, CA W-2 wages)
3. New hire enrollment — add within **30 days** of hire
4. Facilitate payroll deductions — first contribution due 30 days after roster upload
5. Ongoing roster maintenance — manage opt-outs, add employees turning 18
6. No tax reporting burden — IRA trustee files Form 5498
7. No fiduciary liability

## 2. New York — Secure Choice Savings Program

**Market Impact: Very High — Active Greenfield**
**Governing Law:** NY General Business Law, Article 43 (enacted 2021, launched Oct 2025)

### Program
- **Plan Type:** Roth IRA
- **Default Contribution:** 3% of gross wages
- **Employer Contributions:** Prohibited
- **Employee Opt-Out:** Yes, at any time
- **Administrator:** Secure Choice Savings Program Board / third-party administrator
- **Employer Fees:** None

### Eligibility Rules
- **Threshold:** 10+ employees in New York State during prior calendar year
- In business for **at least 2 years**
- Has **not offered a qualified retirement plan** in preceding 2 years
- Part-time and seasonal employees count toward the 10-employee threshold
- Employers with existing qualified plans must still **certify exemption** via portal

### Compliance Deadlines (Rolling — 2026)
| Employer Size | Registration Deadline |
|--------------|----------------------|
| 30+ employees | March 18, 2026 |
| 15–29 employees | May 15, 2026 |
| 10–14 employees | July 15, 2026 |

### Penalties
- As of February 2026, **no formal penalty schedule published**
- Law authorizes civil penalties for failure to register or certify exemption
- Expected: $250+/employee with escalation (education-first approach initially)

### Employer Obligations
1. Register or certify exemption via Secure Choice employer portal (EIN + access code)
2. Submit employee data — name, SSN/ITIN, date of birth, contact info (age 18+, employed in NY)
3. Facilitate payroll deductions — post-tax Roth IRA deductions at 3% default
4. Distribute program information to employees
5. Enroll new hires in timely manner
6. No tax reporting burden — IRA trustee files Form 5498
7. No fiduciary liability

## 3. Illinois — Secure Choice

**Market Impact: High — Mature, Proven Market**
**Governing Law:** Illinois Secure Choice Savings Program Act (820 ILCS 80/)

### Program
- **Plan Type:** Roth IRA (Traditional IRA available for income-ineligible participants)
- **Default Contribution:** 5% of gross pay
- **Employer Contributions:** Prohibited
- **Employee Opt-Out:** Yes, within 30 days of enrollment; can re-enroll anytime
- **Administrator:** Illinois State Treasurer / Ascensus (recordkeeper)
- **Participant Fees:** ~0.75% annual fee on assets

### Eligibility Rules
- **Threshold:** 5+ employees **in every quarter** of the previous calendar year
- In business for **at least 2 years**
- Does **not** offer or contribute to a qualified retirement plan
- Applies to for-profit and nonprofit employers
- Open voluntarily to non-mandated employers and self-employed individuals

### Compliance Deadlines (All Passed)
| Employer Size | Registration Deadline |
|--------------|----------------------|
| 25+ employees | November 1, 2018 |
| 16–24 employees | July 1, 2019 |
| 5–15 employees | November 1, 2023 |

### Penalties
- **Tier I: $250/eligible employee** — first calendar year of non-compliance (assessed 90+ days after notice)
- **Tier II: $500/eligible employee** — each subsequent calendar year
- Employers receive Notice of Proposed Assessment (IDOR-2P-NT) — **120 days** to comply or request hearing
- Compliance within 120-day window avoids the penalty

### Employer Obligations
1. Register at employer.ilsecurechoice.com using employer access code
2. Upload eligible employee roster — all employees age 18+
3. Auto-enroll employees — those who don't opt out within 30 days enrolled at 5% default
4. Facilitate payroll deductions each pay period
5. Enroll new hires within **30 days**
6. Maintain employee records (terminations, new hires, address changes)
7. No fiduciary liability
8. Exempt employers must file exemption at employer.ilsecurechoice.com/exempt

## 4. New Jersey — RetireReady NJ

**Market Impact: High — Enforcement Ramping**
**Governing Law:** NJ Secure Choice Savings Program Act (signed 2019, launched 2024)

### Program
- **Plan Type:** Roth IRA (Traditional IRA option available)
- **Default Contribution:** 3% of gross pay
- **Employer Contributions:** Prohibited
- **Employee Opt-Out:** Yes, at any time
- **Administrator:** NJ Secure Choice Savings Board / third-party administrator
- **Participant Fees:** ~0.75% annual fee on assets

### Eligibility Rules
- **Threshold:** 25+ employees (current law; Assembly Bill A5358 pending — would reduce to 1+)
- Employees counted: age 18+, reside in or employed in NJ, wages subject to NJ income tax
- In business for **at least 2 years**
- Does **not** currently offer a qualified retirement plan
- Both for-profit and nonprofit employers covered
- Exempt employers must still **certify exemption** with RetireReady NJ

### Compliance Deadlines
| Employer Size | Registration Deadline | Penalty Enforcement Begins |
|--------------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| 40+ employees | September 15, 2024 | June 15, 2025 |
| 25–39 employees | November 15, 2024 | August 15, 2025 |

### Penalties
| Year of Non-Compliance | Penalty |
|-----------------------|---------|
| Year 1 | Written warning |
| Year 2 | $100/employee |
| Years 3–4 | $250/employee |
| Year 5+ | $500/employee |

### Employer Obligations
1. Register at RetireReady NJ portal or certify exemption (state-provided access code)
2. Enroll eligible employees within 30 days of enrollment deadline (existing) or 3 months of hire (new)
3. Facilitate payroll deductions — collect and remit per pay period
4. Distribute enrollment materials (state-prepared notification forms)
5. Track opt-outs
6. No employer contributions required
7. No fiduciary liability

## 5. Minnesota — Secure Choice Retirement Program (MNSCRP)

**Market Impact: High — Largest Greenfield Opportunity**
**Governing Law:** Minn. Stat. §§ 187.01–187.11 (enacted May 2023)

### Program
- **Plan Type:** Roth IRA (default); Traditional IRA option available
- **Default Contribution:** 5% of gross pay; auto-escalates 1%/year to 8% cap
- **Employer Contributions:** Prohibited
- **Employee Opt-Out:** Yes, at any time
- **Administrator:** MN Secure Choice Board of Directors / private recordkeeper
- **Employer Fees:** None

### Eligibility Rules
- **Threshold:** 5+ employees (W-2, age 18+)
- In business for **at least 1 year** (12 months)
- Does **not** offer or contribute to a qualified retirement plan (and has not in preceding 12 months)
- Applies to for-profit and nonprofit employers
- Applies even if employees live outside Minnesota

### Compliance Deadlines (Rolling — 2026–2028)
| Employer Size | Registration Deadline |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Soft launch (all sizes voluntary) | January 16 – March 30, 2026 |
| 100+ employees | June 30, 2026 |
| 50–99 employees | December 31, 2026 |
| 10–24 employees | December 31, 2027 |
| 5–9 employees | June 30, 2028 |

### Penalties
| Year of Non-Compliance | Penalty |
|-----------------------|---------|
| Year 1 | Written warning (required by statute) |
| Year 2 | $200/employee (max $6,000) |
| Year 3 | $300/employee (no max) |
| Ongoing | $500/employee (no max) |

Additional: Penalties doubled if employer fails both enrollment AND distribution of required information. Failure to remit withheld deductions within 10 days of demand may result in misdemeanor charges.

### Employer Obligations
1. Register or file exemption via self-service portal (EIN + access code)
2. Distribute program information to employees — at least **30 days** before first paycheck with deductions
3. Auto-enroll eligible employees at 5% default Roth IRA contribution
4. Facilitate payroll deductions
5. Maintain employee records — keep eligibility data current
6. No fiduciary liability
7. Estimated admin burden: ~7 hours/week for contribution management, record updates, and reporting
